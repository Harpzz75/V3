GDPR and Data Privacy are very serious matters!

"GDPR stands for the General Data Protection Regulation. It was introduced in 2018 and applies to all companies and businesses in the EU. What it means is that citizens of the EU have greater control of their personal data and how it is used, stored and destroyed. "Personal data is any information related to a person such as a name, a photo, an email address, bank details, updates on social networking websites, location details, medical information, or a computer IP address." [1]

[1] https://www.superoffice.com/blog/gdpr/

So GDPR might affect digital projects in some different ways.

A company or business must be able to provide a deliverable digital file containing every single piece of data/information which is stored by the company at any given time. Luckily I spent a couple of months on and off working on GDPR for a company who had around 15 clients. Each client needed their own explanation/proof of how how their users data was managed/stored/deleted etc as each clients GDPR requirements were unique. So you can imagine the complexity and size of implementing the correct GDPR strategy for each individual situation, some companies are huge and their GDPR strategy is much more than say a small company which doesn't store a large amount of peoples personal data. A good understanding of each individual client/scenario is essential to create the correct GDPR solution.
