I guess almost all businesses will use email as a main form of communication between staff, customers, suppliers etc. I personally have seen the advancement of communication within businesses flourish, from very early email clients solely for text based messages to the present day array of digital communication like Zoom, Skype and various smart phone technology.

Management systems have been designed and built to help organise the main functions within the business. For example Stock Management Systems to organise and simplify the process of having the right amount of product is stock and monitoring sales. Data Management Systems have again been developed store digital versions of files onto servers for storage which are instantly available wherever a user might be in the world. Good riddance to the good old filing cabinet!

Also different Management Information Systems can be used to track things like sales, productivity, profitability etc and again this information can be accessed digitally anywhere and at anytime. Customer Relationship Management systems can be used to capture and store every customer interaction on a certain app or website and this data can then be used to improve the customer journey and maybe increase sales.
